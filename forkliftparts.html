Before operating a forklift, it's good to know the Parts. The Forklift Parts in general and their functions are as follows:

  • Mast

    • The mast is the vertical support that permits raising and lowering the load.

  • Lift/Tilt Cylinder

    • Controls the amount by which the mast structure, forks and carriage may be tilted beyond the vertical position, forward or backward.

  • Load Backrest

    • Attached to the carriage and prevents the load shifting backwards, it protects the mast cylinders hoses as well as preventing any goods falling through the mast and hitting the operator.

  • Forks

    • Another part of forklift is the fork which is located at the very bottom. Forks have functions to carry and lift materials and move them from one place to another.

  • Carriege

    • A support structure where the forks are attached or mounted.

  • Drive Wheel

    • The position of the drive wheels on the forklift is on the tires. In general, there are two types of tires, such as air tires and solid tires. Solid tires used on uneven road terrain.

  • Steer Wheel

    • Steer wheels are a mechanism for moving that is assisted by hydraulic so that the operator becomes lighter in turning the rear wheels.

  • Operator Cab

    • This chair is intended to make it easier for the operator and to make them feel comfortable, so that in doing the job the operator feels more focused.

  • Engine

    • The engine compartment on a sit-down internal combustion forklift is located directly under the operatorâ€™s seat.

  • Battery Compartment

    • The battery compartment on an electric forklift is also located under the seat.

  • Counter Weight

    • The weight installed by the manufacturer to give stability to the machine under load.

  • Overhead Guard

    • A framework/roof, providing overhead protection for the operator from falling objects.
